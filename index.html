Jimzo, meaning “clay art” (with jim meaning clay and zo meaning art), is one of Bhutan’s oldest and most sacred traditional crafts. Its origins trace back to the 7th century during the time of Tibetan King Songtsen Gampo, when pioneering temples such as Kichu Lhakhang and Jampa Lhakhang were built. Evidence of early clay paintings from the time of Guru Rinpoche further shows that clay sculpting existed in Bhutan long before it was officially recorded. The art became more widespread during the time of Terton Pema Lingpa in the 15th century, and later, in the 17th century, it was formally codified as one of the Zorig Chusum the thirteen traditional arts and crafts of Bhutan by the Fourth Druk Desi, Gyalse Tenzin Rabgye.


During the 17th century, Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal invited the great sculptor Trulku Dzing to Bhutan, who created remarkable clay figures in Punakha and Wangduephodrang Dzongs. Since then, the techniques of jimzo have been passed down from master sculptors (Jimzo Lopoen) to students for generations. Bhutanese sculptors are well-known for their delicate craftsmanship, strict precision, and deep devotion, as religious clay sculptures must follow exact iconographic measurements for the body, limbs, facial features, ornaments, and attire. Creating these statues is considered a highly spiritual act, believed to bring merit and blessings.

My Project

When I look at my model, I see more than just a sculpture; I see a visual journey that brings together everything I learned about meditation. At first, it may appear to be a simple meditating figure, but for me, every part of it carries meaning. The calm posture of the figure shows the inner stillness and clarity that I came to understand through this project, while the Dharma Wheel beneath it immediately connects the model to the deeper teachings of Buddhism. Together, these elements create a full picture of what meditation represents. I also see the model as a blend of the old and the new. The figure is shaped in a modern style, representing someone like me or anyone living today who is trying to find balance in a busy world. In contrast, the Dharma Wheel symbolizes ancient wisdom that has guided people for generations. By combining the two, my model shows how meditation still remains meaningful and relevant, no matter how much time has passed. To me, the sculpture is a reminder that meditation is not just about sitting quietly. It is supported by many aspects of mindfulness, ethics, discipline, and understanding just like the Dharma Wheel supports the figure. When I look at it this way, I see meditation as something much bigger: not just a practice, but a way of living with awareness, kindness, and purpose. Overall, I see my model as a symbol of harmony. It brings together focus and openness, modern life and tradition, and personal well-being with the values of my community. Creating it helped me understand meditation on a deeper level, and now the finished model continues to represent that learning every time I see it.

Significance of my Project

The meditating figure at the center of the model represents the core idea of meditation. Its calm and balanced posture expresses inner peace, mental clarity, and emotional stability. The way the figure sits reflects both major forms of meditation: Focused Attention is shown through the slightly directed posture that suggests concentration on a single point, while the relaxed and open positioning of the body represents Open Monitoring, where one simply observes thoughts without judgment. Together, the figure shows that meditation is a blend of focus and openness, discipline and gentleness.

The model is built on a Dharma Wheel, which adds deeper meaning to the entire structure. The wheel carries the symbolism of the Noble Eightfold Path through its eight spokes, reminding us that meditation is part of a larger path of wisdom and understanding. At the center of the wheel, the hub represents moral discipline. This is the foundation that keeps the whole path steady, showing that ethical behavior and compassion support a strong meditative practice. Surrounding the spokes is the rim, which stands for mindful concentration. Just as the rim holds the wheel together, mindfulness helps maintain balance and clarity in daily life. Placing a modern figure on top of this ancient Buddhist symbol shows the connection between traditional teachings and present-day well-being. It reflects how meditation continues to remain relevant, helping individuals build inner peace while also promoting harmony in the community. The design brings together the past and the present, suggesting that these teachings still guide people today. Overall, the structure of the sculpture shows meditation as a complete experience that includes focus, peace, ethics, and mindfulness. The combination of the calm figure and the symbolic wheel expresses how creating the model was not just an artistic task but an experience that deepened understanding. The model captures the idea that meditation is not only studied but also lived, practiced, and felt.
